Nutella Macarons

Home

Description

This is another sweet recipe that I get alot of compliments on! This recipe mainly focuses on creating yummy chocolate macaron shells, the Nutella is spread in-between the two shells.

The recipe can be done using either the french method or italian method to create the meringue. The instructions below specify the french method. The italian method is more complex to do, but creates a more stable meringue that prevents cracking and flat macaron shells.

The french method adds caster sugar gradually to the egg whites. The italian method makes a sugar syrup from the caster sugar and water, which must be boiled to a specific temperature. Please refer to this instruction to complete this recipe with the italian method (change egg whites to 190g egg whites split into 95g each).

Ingredients

  • 6 egg whites (or 190g if using italian method)
  • 260g (2cups) icing sugar
  • 240g (2 1/4 cups) ground almond
  • 30g (1/4 cup) cocoa powder
  • 200g (1 cup) caster sugar
  • Nutella

Steps

  1. Sieve and mix together the icing sugar, ground almonds, and cocoa powder.
  2. Beat the egg whites in a bowl; when they are frothy, add half the caster sugar and continue to beat. When they start to become stiff, add the remaining sugar and continue beating to make a thick meringue mixture.
  3. Using a spatula, fold in the sieved ingredients. Use large, gentle movements for at least 2 minutes, working the mixture from the edges of the bowl towards the centre. The mixture should form a ribbon when it falls from the spoon.
  4. Preheat oven to 135 degrees celsius.
  5. Cover a baking sheet with baking paper. Pour the mixture into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle and pipe it onto the parchment in rounds of diameter 3.5cm. Set aside for 20 minutes in a dry place until a crust forms on the surface.
  6. Before putting the macarons in the oven, place the baking sheet on top of another baking sheet; preventing the bottom of the macarons to be overcooked. Bake for 15 minutes. Touch the top of the macaron and try to wiggle it, if the shell moves or slides on its "feet", it is underbaked and requires 1-2 more miinutes.
  7. Leave the macarons to cool for 5-10 minutes then remove from the baking sheet. When cold, spread half with Nutella and sandwhich together with the other halves. Refrigerate for 24 hours before serving so that the macarons soften.
