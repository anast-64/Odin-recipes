Galaktoboureko

Home

Description

This is a recipe for a yummy greek custard pie. This is my family's special recipe that has been passed down through generations. Whenever I make this cake I always get compliments and people going in for second and thirds!

The custard is created from scratch, along with the sweet citrus syrup that is poured on top of the cake. Please take care to let the cake set for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the custard to set properly. If the cake is cut too soon, the slices will have no structure and turn into tasty sweet mush. I always start by setting the syrup to boil before focusing on the custard.

Ingredients

Syrup

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Cake

  • 1 1/2 cups Sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups Semolina
  • 2 litres Milk
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla sugar or vanilla extract
  • 100g unsalted butter (for custard)
  • 375g Antoniou Pastry Fillo
  • 150g unsalted butter

Steps

Syrup

  1. Put water and sugar in pot over high heat to boil.
  2. Squeeze some lemon juice into the pot (approximately a table spoon).
  3. Cut some lemon rind and place in pot along with a cinnaom stick.
  4. Once boiling lower heat to medium-low and let syrup simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Turn off heat once finished and leave to cool.

Cake

  1. Put sugar, semolina, eggs, milk, and vanilla sugar/extract in a large pot.
  2. Mix and place pot on stove with high heat and continue mixing (NOTE: it is important that you keep mixing the pot while it is cooking so the semolina doesn't stick to the bottom of the pot and burn).
  3. Once heated, turn down heat to medium.
  4. Continue mixing until custard thickens, once thick like custard consistency (creamy and smooth) turn off heat, add butter and continue mixing.
  5. Melt remaining butter and use pastry brush to paint bottom and sides of deep baking tray with melted butter.
  6. Separate fillo pastry into halve to make two stacks of pastry. One will be used for the bottom half of the cake and the rest will go on top of the custard.
  7. Pre-heat oven to 180 degrees celsius.
  8. Cut Galaktoboureko by first cutting in half horizontally, then cutting those horizontal sections in half. You should have 4 horizontal sections. Cut diagonally across each section to make smaller pieces (see picture).
  9. Put in oven and bake for 1 hour.
  10. Once baked, make sure the syrup is cooled before pouring slowly over Galaktoboureko make sure to cover every inch of the cake (including edges and corners).
  11. Leave to set for 2-3 hours before cutting along the cuts made before baking and serving.
